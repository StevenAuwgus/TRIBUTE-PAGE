Bob Marley

The legend of reggae music

Bob Marley was a Jamaican singer-songwriter whose distillation of early ska, rock steady, and reggae musical forms blossomed in the 1970s into an electrifying rock-influenced hybrid that made him an international superstar.

Here's a time line of Bob Marley's life:

  • 1945:Born in St Ann Parish, Jamaica.
  • 1962:Haile Selassie, emperor of Ethiopia and worshipped by the Rastafari, visits Jamaica. Many, many Rastafari were present, including Bob.
  • 1963:Bob Marley and the Wailers was formed when Bob Marley and Bunney Wailer met Peter Tosh. Originally a ska band, they evolved into a Jamacan reggae band over time.
  • April 21, 1966:Bob's mother remarried and moved to America, leaving him alone in Jamaica. He began to socialize with those of the Rastan faith, and although raised Catholic he adopted the Rastafari Religion.
  • December 3, 1976:After agreeing to perform a peace concert in Jamaica to try to settle down political tension, Bob's home was assaulted by armed men who shot at him, his wife, and his manager. He escaped with few injuries, and while both of the others recieved serious wounds, they all survived.
  • 1977:Bob was diagnosed with melanoma in his toe. Though doctors recommended he amputate his entire leg, he followed the advice of his family and friends and only removed the infected part of the toe.
  • April 16, 1980:Bob arrived in Zimbabwe to play a concert there, one encouraging the movement to liberate the black people there.
  • May 11, 1981:Bob died in Miami, Florida from cancer that he had been fighting for four years. Though his treatment in Germany had allowed him to fight it for many months, it had become apparent that he didn't have long to live. He set out to visit Jamaica one last time, but unfortunately didn't make it.
